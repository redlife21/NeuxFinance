Usage

Bollinger Bands are an indicator often used in technical analysis to measure volatility. There are several basic principles for interpretation: Expansion and Contraction of the Bands: When the bands are widening, it means that the market is volatile. When the bands narrow, it indicates low volatility and a tightening market, which can often be a precursor to a large price move. Price Touching the Bands: Price touching the upper band indicates high buying pressure, while price touching the lower band indicates high selling pressure. However, this should not be automatically interpreted as a buy or sell signal. Out-of-Band Movements: When prices move outside the Bollinger Bands, it indicates that the market is overbought (upper band) or oversold (lower band). These situations may signal the possibility of a correction. Bollinger Jam: A situation where the price moves within a narrow channel after which a sharp price movement is usually observed. Trends and Bands: If the price moves permanently above the bands, it can be interpreted as a bullish trend; if it moves permanently below it, it can be interpreted as a bearish trend.
